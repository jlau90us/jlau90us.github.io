The Rise of Digital

There is a lot of ambiguity around what the term "digital" means in how it is used today. The term Digital was born out of the rise of computers and the processing power that came with it. In order for computers to process information, it had to be stored in a digital format that was recognizable by machine language and computer code.

Advances in sensor technology has created a boom of digital information and when combined with the increased availability of wireless access to the internet, an incredible need to store and process data was created.

In the most recent days, a new invention known as cloud computing has allowed unprecedented storage and processing of data through cloud computing algorithms that enable massive parallel processing and storage across an unlimited number of computers, servers, and data centers.

This seemingly limitless amount of storage and processing capabilities has also now unlocked new methods in data analytics. Where complex simulations on massive data sets used to take months to process, cloud computing can return results in just minutes or even seconds. This advance has now unlocked the real possibility of applying artificial intelligence and machine learning to understanding data in a new way.

While there are still challenges in how we employ these technologies broadly, it is an incredibly exciting time because we these new advances in AI and cloud computing are driving new discoveries into scientific research and productivity, and redefining the realm of what is possible.
